The Project Pepitas

The primary goal of the Pepitas project was to discover the fundamental concepts of cryptocurrencies by recreating one in C language and developping all the tools and interfaces that are necessary for users to use it.

The initial idea was to develop a lighter version, similar to Peercoin, i.e. a proof-of-stake-based currency (PoS) rather than Proof of Work (PoW), which is the consensus used, in particular, by Bitcoin.

How to Use

Once you've downloaded and started Pepitas, you'll have access to a Login window in the user's interface. If it's your first time using it, you'll have to create an account (Be sure to remember your password, it must be at least 5 characters long). Then, or if you already have an account, you can login.

Now that you're logged in, you can see your wallet, it has a lot of important informations in it. First of all, your balance, aka the number of pepitas you posses is showned at your right. Below, are displayed your public and private keys. For your own security, the second one is covered up and revealed when the button is pressed, be careful when you need to see it.

Your wallet also shows the numbers of connections to the server and the mempool; the number of transactions pending. Finally, the progress bar represents the purcentage of synchronisation with the blockchain.

The others windows, Transaction, Validator, Contacts and Transaction History respectifully allow you to: make a transaction a of certain amount of Pepitas to a user given his key, invest (and recover your investissement) in Pepitas to increase your chances of becoming a validator (see Proof of Stake in the Report), manage your contacts and see your pasts transactions.
